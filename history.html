The items in the list above bear little resemblance to each other. Some are confirmed to be suicides, whereas others, speculations. Some suicides were due to the immense social pressure, whereas others, the protecting of their interests. There are no records on the first person who has committed suicide, neither are there records of the total number of victims to this menace. However, there are several cases which have made a bigger impact on the world than others.

Feelings, whether arising from a medical condition or the situation one is in, have been one important factor in the decision of one committing suicide. This has been seen in many cases, such as that of famous artist Vincent van Gogh, famous for his cheerful paintings of sunflowers. Other cases, such as the spate of suicide pacts in Japan can also be attributed to the social condition of the people who committed suicide. These people, termed as hikikomori 9 10, or social outcasts. They withdraw from the society into their own recluse, both mentally and physically, cooping themselves up in rooms to avoid social contact, leading to abnormal tendencies, such as suicide and the assaulting of parents.

Honour has been another important factor. This has been seen in cases such as that of the kamikaze pilots who sacrificed their lives to protect their own, or the nation¡¯s, interests. More recently, suicide bombers have made a comeback, in the form of non-governmental political groups which have been tagged as terrorists.
